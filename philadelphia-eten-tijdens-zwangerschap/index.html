What is Philadelphia cheese?

Philadelphia cheese is a type of cream cheese that is known for its smooth and creamy texture. It is commonly used as a spread on bagels and as an ingredient in recipes like cheesecake. Philadelphia cheese is made from fresh milk and cream, and it is known for its mild and slightly tangy flavor.

History and Myth of Philadelphia cheese during pregnancy

There is a common myth that pregnant women should avoid soft cheeses like Philadelphia cheese due to the risk of listeria contamination. Listeria is a type of bacteria that can cause serious infections in pregnant women, leading to complications such as miscarriage, stillbirth, and preterm labor. However, this myth is not entirely accurate. While it is true that some soft cheeses can be contaminated with listeria, Philadelphia cheese is generally considered safe to eat during pregnancy.

Recommendation of Philadelphia cheese during pregnancy

Based on the recommendations of the American Pregnancy Association, pregnant women can safely consume pasteurized soft cheeses like Philadelphia cheese.
