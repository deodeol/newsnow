What is American Flag Stars and Stripes?

The American flag stars and stripes are the two key elements of the United States flag. The stars, arranged in a blue field called the canton, represent the states of the country. Each star represents a state, and the number of stars has increased over time as new states were admitted to the Union. The stripes, alternating between red and white, represent the original thirteen colonies that declared independence from Britain and formed the United States.

History and Myth of American Flag Stars and Stripes

The history of the American flag stars and stripes dates back to the Revolutionary War. The stars and stripes design was first introduced by the Continental Congress in 1777, and it has evolved over the years. The American flag stars and stripes have become a powerful symbol of American identity and pride.
