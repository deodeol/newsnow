The vertical stripes on the American flag are a crucial part of its design. There are thirteen stripes in total, alternating between red and white. History and Myth of Vertical Stripes on American Flag

The design of the American flag with vertical stripes dates back to the late 18th century. The flag's stripes were originally inspired by the British Union Jack, which featured horizontal stripes. However, the American flag's vertical stripes were chosen to differentiate it from the British flag and symbolize the new nation's independence. Over time, the vertical stripes have become a powerful symbol of American identity and unity.
