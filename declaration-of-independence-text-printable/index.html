Thomas Jefferson, the primary author of the Declaration of Independence, drew inspiration from Enlightenment thinkers such as John Locke and Jean-Jacques Rousseau. The document reflects the ideals of individual rights, government by consent, and the right to revolution if the government fails to protect those rights.
