Aside from its natural beauty, Coney Island also holds a rich history and myth. The island was once owned by the Haw Par brothers, who were famous for their Tiger Balm ointment. They transformed the island into a recreational park in the 1930s, complete with a hotel, zoo, and even a golf course. However, due to financial difficulties, the park eventually closed down and the island was left abandoned for many years.

Today, Coney Island has been restored to its natural state and is managed by the National Parks Board. The island offers a range of recreational activities, such as cycling, hiking, and picnicking. There are also several lookout points where visitors can enjoy panoramic views of the surrounding nature.
