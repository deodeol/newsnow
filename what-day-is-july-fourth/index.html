What Day is July 4th?

July 4th is known as Independence Day in the United States. It is a national holiday that commemorates the signing of the Declaration of Independence on July 4, 1776. This document declared the thirteen American colonies as a new nation, free from British rule. It is a day that symbolizes freedom, liberty, and the birth of the United States of America.

The History and Myth of July 4th

The history of July 4th dates back to the American Revolutionary War, which began in 1775. The war was fought between Great Britain and the thirteen American colonies, who were seeking independence. On July 2, 1776, the Second Continental Congress voted in favor of independence from Britain. Two days later, on July 4th, the final draft of the Declaration of Independence was adopted, officially declaring the United States as an independent nation. However, it is worth noting that the actual signing of the document took place on August 2, 1776.
