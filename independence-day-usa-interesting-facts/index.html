Every year on July 4th, people all across the United States celebrate Independence Day. It's a time for barbecues, fireworks, and spending time with loved ones. But do you know the interesting facts behind this holiday? In this blog post, we will explore the history, myths, hidden secrets, and fun facts of Independence Day. So, let's dive in and discover the fascinating world of this patriotic celebration!

What is Independence Day?

Independence Day, also known as the Fourth of July, is a federal holiday in the United States commemorating the Declaration of Independence of the United States on July 4, 1776. It is a day to celebrate the birth of the United States and the freedom and independence that came with it.

History and Myth

The history of Independence Day dates back to the American Revolution, when the thirteen colonies declared themselves independent from British rule. The Continental Congress approved the final wording of the Declaration of Independence on July 4, 1776. However, contrary to popular belief, the Declaration was not signed on that day. The actual signing took place on August 2, 1776.

There are also many myths and legends surrounding Independence Day. One popular myth is that Betsy Ross sewed the first American flag. While there is no concrete evidence to support this claim, it has become a cherished part of American folklore.

Hidden Secrets

Did you know that the Liberty Bell, one of the most iconic symbols of American independence, is actually not rung on Independence Day? The bell is considered too fragile to be rung, so it is tapped 13 times to represent the original thirteen colonies. Another hidden secret is that fireworks have been a part of Independence Day celebrations since the very first anniversary in 1777.

Recommendation

If you're looking to celebrate Independence Day in a unique way, why not visit Philadelphia, where the Declaration of Independence was signed? You can tour Independence Hall and see where history was made. Another recommendation is to attend a local parade or fireworks display. These events bring communities together and create a sense of patriotism and unity.

How to Celebrate Independence Day

There are countless ways to celebrate Independence Day. You can host a backyard barbecue with friends and family, complete with burgers, hot dogs, and all the fixings. Or, you can organize a neighborhood block party with games, music, and food. If you're feeling adventurous, you can even plan a weekend getaway to a beach or lake and enjoy some fun in the sun.

Fun Facts about Independence Day

1. The original draft of the Declaration of Independence was written by Thomas Jefferson, but it went through several revisions before being approved.

2. John Adams, the second President of the United States, believed that Independence Day should be celebrated on July 2nd, the day the Continental Congress voted for independence.

3. The oldest continuous Independence Day celebration in the United States is held in Bristol, Rhode Island. The tradition dates back to 1785.

4. The Statue of Liberty, a symbol of freedom and democracy, was a gift from France to the United States. It was dedicated on October 28, 1886.

Now that you know these interesting facts about Independence Day, you can impress your friends and family with your knowledge. So, go out and celebrate this patriotic holiday with pride and gratitude for the freedoms we enjoy as Americans. Happy Independence Day!
