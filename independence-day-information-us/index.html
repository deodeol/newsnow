Independence Day, also known as the Fourth of July, is a federal holiday in the United States that commemorates the adoption of the Declaration of Independence on July 4, 1776. It is a day to celebrate the birth of the United States and the freedom and independence that it represents.

The target of Independence Day in the US is to educate people about the significance of this historic event and to honor the Founding Fathers who fought for freedom and independence. It is also a time for Americans to come together and celebrate their patriotism and love for their country.

What is Independence Day?

Independence Day is the day when the United States officially declared its independence from British rule. It marks the beginning of the American Revolution and the birth of the United States as a sovereign nation. On July 4, 1776, the Continental Congress adopted the Declaration of Independence, which declared the thirteen American colonies free and independent from British rule.

Independence Day is a time to reflect on the values and principles that the United States was founded upon, such as freedom, equality, and democracy. It is a day to honor the brave men and women who fought for these ideals and to celebrate the progress and achievements of the nation.

History and Myth of Independence Day

The history of Independence Day dates back to the American Revolution, when the thirteen American colonies revolted against British rule. The colonists believed that they were being unfairly taxed and governed without representation, leading to widespread protests and acts of resistance.

One popular myth associated with Independence Day is that the Liberty Bell in Philadelphia was rung to announce the adoption of the Declaration of Independence. However, there is no historical evidence to support this claim. The Liberty Bell did not ring on July 4, 1776, but it did become a symbol of American independence in the years following the Revolution.

Hidden Secrets of Independence Day

One hidden secret of Independence Day is the fact that the original copy of the Declaration of Independence is not on display in the National Archives Museum. Instead, it is kept in a climate-controlled vault for preservation purposes. The document is extremely fragile and is only displayed for a short period of time each year.

Another hidden secret is the fact that the Declaration of Independence was not signed on July 4, 1776. While the document was adopted on that day, it was not actually signed until August 2, 1776. The famous signatures that we see on the document today were added at a later date.
