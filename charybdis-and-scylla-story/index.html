Charybdis and Scylla Story: Explained

Charybdis and Scylla were two mythical sea monsters that inhabited the Strait of Messina, a narrow passage of water between Italy and Sicily. Charybdis was a giant whirlpool that would swallow ships whole, while Scylla was a six-headed monster that would snatch sailors from the decks of passing vessels. These two creatures were positioned on opposite sides of the strait, making it nearly impossible for sailors to avoid their deadly grasp.
