Understanding UK Interest Rates

Understanding UK interest rates is key to making informed financial decisions. Interest rates affect everything from savings accounts to mortgages, so it's important to have a basic understanding of how they work.

At its simplest, an interest rate is the cost of borrowing money or the return on an investment. When you borrow money, you pay interest on the amount borrowed. When you invest money, you earn interest on the amount invested. The interest rate is expressed as a percentage and can be fixed or variable.

Interest rates are influenced by a variety of factors, including inflation, economic growth, and government policy. When the economy is growing, interest rates tend to rise to help prevent inflation. When the economy is slowing, interest rates tend to fall to encourage borrowing and spending.

By understanding how UK interest rates work, you can make more informed decisions about your finances and investments.
