The SAFF Championship, also known as the South Asian Football Federation Championship, is a biennial football tournament held among the South Asian countries. Since its inception in 1993, the SAFF Championship has become one of the most prestigious football tournaments in the region, showcasing the talents and skills of South Asian footballers.

Over the years, the SAFF Championship has witnessed intense rivalries, thrilling matches, and moments of triumph for the participating nations. However, the history of the tournament is not without its challenges and setbacks.

The SAFF Championship was established with the aim of promoting football in South Asia and providing a platform for the national teams to compete against each other. The tournament serves as a qualifying event for the AFC Asian Cup, with the winner earning a spot in the prestigious tournament.

Throughout its history, the SAFF Championship has seen teams from Bangladesh, India, Maldives, Nepal, Pakistan, and Sri Lanka vying for the title. These countries have produced some exceptional players who have gone on to achieve success at the international level.

The SAFF Championship has a history that dates back to 1993 when the first edition of the tournament was held in Bangladesh. Since then, the tournament has been hosted by different countries in the South Asian region, providing an opportunity for football fans to witness top-class football action.
