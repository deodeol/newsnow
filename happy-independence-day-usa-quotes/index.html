What is Independence Day?

Independence Day, also known as the Fourth of July, is a federal holiday in the United States that commemorates the adoption of the Declaration of Independence on July 4, 1776. This document declared the thirteen American colonies as a new nation, free from British rule. It marked the birth of the United States of America as an independent country.

History and Myth of Independence Day

The history of Independence Day dates back to the American Revolution, when the colonists fought for their freedom from British rule. The Declaration of Independence, drafted by Thomas Jefferson, was a pivotal moment in this fight for independence. It proclaimed the colonies' right to self-governance and laid the groundwork for the formation of a new nation.

Hidden Secrets of Independence Day

While Independence Day is a time of celebration, there are also hidden secrets and untold stories behind this historic event. For example, did you know that the original draft of the Declaration of Independence was written on hemp paper? Or that John Adams, one of the Founding Fathers, believed that Independence Day should be celebrated on July 2nd, the day the Continental Congress voted in favor of independence?
