The History and Myth of Philadelphia Chromosome-Negative CML Treatment

The history of Philadelphia chromosome-negative CML treatment dates back to the discovery of the Philadelphia chromosome in the 1960s. This genetic abnormality was found to be present in the majority of CML cases and led to the development of targeted therapies such as tyrosine kinase inhibitors. However, it was later discovered that a subset of CML cases did not have the Philadelphia chromosome mutation, leading to the identification of Philadelphia chromosome-negative CML. Despite the initial belief that these cases were more difficult to treat, advancements in targeted therapies and other treatment modalities have shown promising results in managing this condition effectively.
