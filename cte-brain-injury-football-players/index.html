What is CTE brain injury?

CTE brain injury is a progressive degenerative brain disease that is caused by repeated blows to the head. It is commonly found in athletes who participate in contact sports, such as football, boxing, and hockey. The repeated trauma causes a buildup of an abnormal protein called tau, which forms tangles in the brain and eventually leads to the death of brain cells. The symptoms of CTE brain injury can vary, but often include memory loss, confusion, depression, and aggression.
