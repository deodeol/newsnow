What is the SAFF Championship?

The SAFF Championship, also known as the South Asian Football Federation Championship, is a biennial football tournament organized by the South Asian Football Federation (SAFF). The tournament features national teams from South Asia, including India, Bangladesh, Nepal, Sri Lanka, Maldives, Bhutan, Afghanistan, and Pakistan. The SAFF Championship provides a platform for these teams to showcase their skills and compete for regional glory.

History and Myth of SAFF Championship

The first SAFF Championship was held in 1993 in Lahore, Pakistan, with India emerging as the winners. Since then, the tournament has been held every two years, with each edition hosted by a different member country. Over the years, the SAFF Championship has witnessed some intense rivalries, memorable matches, and outstanding performances by players from the region. The tournament has become a significant event in the football calendar of South Asia, attracting millions of fans and generating immense excitement.
