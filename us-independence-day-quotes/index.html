One of the little-known facts about US Independence Day is that it was not actually declared on July 4th. The Continental Congress voted to declare independence on July 2nd, but the final draft of the Declaration of Independence was approved on July 4th. This is why we celebrate on July 4th.

Did you know that the original Declaration of Independence is housed in the National Archives in Washington, D.C.? It is carefully preserved and protected to ensure its longevity. This document is a powerful symbol of our nation's birth and the ideals it stands for.

Fun Facts about US Independence Day Quotes

1. The first celebration of US Independence Day took place on July 8, 1776, in Philadelphia. The city was illuminated with candles and bonfires, and there were parades, concerts, and fireworks.

2. The oldest continuous Independence Day celebration in the United States is held in Bristol, Rhode Island. The town has been celebrating the Fourth of July since 1785.

3. The largest fireworks display in the United States is held on the National Mall in Washington, D.C. It attracts hundreds of thousands of spectators each year.

4. The Liberty Bell, an iconic symbol of American independence, is rung every year on July 4th at Independence Hall in Philadelphia. The bell is tapped 13 times to honor the original 13 colonies.
