The History and Myth of the 4th of July 2023 calendar

The 4th of July is a significant date in American history. It marks the day when the United States officially declared its independence from Great Britain in 1776. The signing of the Declaration of Independence by the Founding Fathers paved the way for the birth of a new nation, built on the principles of freedom and democracy.

Over the years, several myths and legends have emerged surrounding the 4th of July. One popular myth is that Betsy Ross sewed the first American flag. While there is no concrete evidence to support this claim, it has become a part of the nation's folklore.

The Hidden Secrets of the 4th of July 2023 calendar

Did you know that the 4th of July was not always celebrated on the 4th? In fact, the first celebrations took place on July 8th, 1776, when the Declaration of Independence was read to the public for the first time. It wasn't until 1870 that Congress declared the 4th of July a national holiday.

Another hidden secret of the 4th of July is that the Liberty Bell was not rung on that day. Contrary to popular belief, the iconic bell was actually rung on July 8th, 1776, to mark the first public reading of the Declaration of Independence.
