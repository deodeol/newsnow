The History and Myth of Independence Day

On July 4th, 1776, the Continental Congress adopted the Declaration of Independence, declaring that the thirteen American colonies were no longer part of the British Empire. This historic document laid the foundation for the United States of America and established the principles of freedom, liberty, and equality.

However, there are many myths and legends surrounding the 4th of July. For example, did you know that the Liberty Bell wasn't actually rung on July 4th, 1776? In fact, it wasn't even called the Liberty Bell until the 1830s! These stories and more add to the rich history and lore of Independence Day.

Fun Facts about Independence Day

Did you know that the 4th of July wasn't declared a federal holiday until 1941? Or that three U.S. presidents (John Adams, Thomas Jefferson, and James Monroe) died on July 4th? These are just a few of the fun facts that make Independence Day even more special.
