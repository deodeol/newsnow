What is Independence Day United States Celebration?

Independence Day in the United States is a national holiday that commemorates the signing of the Declaration of Independence on July 4, 1776. This document, drafted by Thomas Jefferson and adopted by the Second Continental Congress, declared the thirteen American colonies to be independent of British rule. The day is celebrated with various activities, including parades, fireworks, concerts, and family gatherings. It is a time for Americans to come together and express their patriotism and gratitude for the freedoms they enjoy.

History and Myth of Independence Day United States Celebration

The history of Independence Day dates back to the American Revolution, when the thirteen colonies were fighting for their independence from Britain. The Continental Congress, composed of delegates from each colony, voted to declare independence on July 2, 1776. Two days later, on July 4th, the Declaration of Independence was formally adopted. While the signing of the document did not actually occur on this day, July 4th has been recognized as the official day of celebration. Over the years, the significance of this day has grown, and it has become a symbol of American patriotism and freedom.

Hidden Secrets of Independence Day United States Celebration

While Independence Day is widely known for its fireworks, barbecues, and parades, there are some hidden secrets and lesser-known facts about this celebration. One such secret is the fact that only two men actually signed the Declaration of Independence on July 4th, 1776: John Hancock and Charles Thomson. The rest of the delegates added their signatures over the following months. Another secret is that the original draft of the Declaration was lost, and the version we know today is a copy made by a printer named John Dunlap. These hidden secrets add an element of intrigue and mystery to the celebration.
