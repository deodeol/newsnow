Israel vs Palestine Timeline: Explained

The Israel vs Palestine conflict dates back to the late 19th century when Jewish and Arab nationalist movements emerged in the region. The conflict revolves around competing claims to the land, religious tensions, historical grievances, and geopolitical interests. The timeline of this conflict encompasses key events such as the Balfour Declaration in 1917, the United Nations Partition Plan in 1947, the establishment of the State of Israel in 1948, multiple wars and uprisings, peace negotiations, and ongoing territorial disputes. This timeline is a reflection of the complex and multifaceted nature of the conflict.

The History and Myth of Israel vs Palestine Timeline

The history of the Israel vs Palestine conflict is intertwined with myths and narratives from both sides. These myths often shape the perceptions and beliefs of the people involved, making it challenging to find common ground for resolution. For Israelis, the establishment of the State of Israel in 1948 is seen as a fulfillment of a historical and religious promise. On the other hand, Palestinians perceive the creation of Israel as a dispossession and displacement of their homeland. Understanding these historical and mythological narratives is crucial to comprehending the deep-rooted nature of the conflict.
