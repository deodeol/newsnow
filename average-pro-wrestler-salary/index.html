The History and Myth of Average Pro Wrestler Salary

Throughout the history of pro wrestling, there have been many myths and misconceptions surrounding the salaries of wrestlers. One common myth is that all pro wrestlers are millionaires, living a life of luxury and excess. While it's true that some top stars in major promotions can earn substantial sums of money, the reality is that the majority of wrestlers struggle to make a decent living.

In the early days of pro wrestling, wrestlers were often paid a percentage of the gate receipts, meaning that their earnings were directly tied to the success of the event. This system often led to financial instability, as wrestlers had no guarantee of income from one event to the next. Today, many wrestlers are paid a flat fee for their appearances, which provides a more stable income but can still be unpredictable.
