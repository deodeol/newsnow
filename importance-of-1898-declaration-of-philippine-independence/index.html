Fun Facts about the 1898 Declaration of Philippine Independence

- The 1898 Declaration of Philippine Independence was first read aloud by General Emilio Aguinaldo from the balcony of his home in Kawit, Cavite. - The declaration was written by Ambrosio Rianzares Bautista, a lawyer and member of the Malolos Congress. - The original copy of the declaration is currently housed in the National Library of the Philippines. - The declaration was not recognized by the international community until the Treaty of Paris was signed in 1898, officially ending the Spanish-American War and recognizing Philippine independence. - The declaration was reenacted annually on June 12th until it was declared a national holiday, known as Independence Day, in 1962.
