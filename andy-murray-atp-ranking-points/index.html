What are ATP ranking points?

ATP ranking points are a numerical value assigned to professional tennis players based on their performance in ATP tournaments. These points determine the player's position in the ATP rankings, which is a list of the top male tennis players in the world.

Ranking points are awarded based on a player's performance in individual matches and tournaments. The number of points awarded depends on the round reached in the tournament and the ranking of the opponent. Higher-ranked opponents and more prestigious tournaments offer more points.

For example, a victory against a higher-ranked opponent in a Grand Slam tournament would earn a player more ranking points than a victory against a lower-ranked opponent in a smaller tournament.

Players accumulate ranking points over a rolling 52-week period. Points earned in a tournament are removed from a player's total after 52 weeks, which means players must continually perform well to maintain or improve their ranking.

The History and Myth of ATP ranking points

The ATP ranking system was introduced in August 1973 to provide a standardized method of ranking male tennis players. The system has undergone several changes and updates over the years to adapt to the evolving landscape of professional tennis.
