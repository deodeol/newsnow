What is Independence Day?

Independence Day, also known as Hari Kemerdekaan, is a national holiday in Indonesia commemorating the country's independence from Dutch colonial rule. On August 17, 1945, Indonesia declared its independence from the Netherlands, marking the beginning of a long and arduous journey towards self-governance. Independence Day is celebrated annually on August 17th with various activities and festivities throughout the country.

The History and Myth of Independence Day

The history of Indonesia's struggle for independence is filled with brave men and women who fought tirelessly against colonial rule. One of the most prominent figures in this fight was Sukarno, who later became Indonesia's first president. The myth surrounding Independence Day revolves around the idea that Indonesia's independence was achieved through the unity and determination of its people, despite the challenges and hardships they faced.
