The Declaration of Independence is a document that was adopted by the Second Continental Congress on July 4, 1776. It declared that the thirteen American colonies were no longer under British rule and were now independent states. This historic document laid the foundation for the formation of the United States of America and is considered one of the most important documents in American history.

The target of the Declaration of Independence was to assert the rights of the American colonies and to justify their decision to break away from British rule. The document lists a series of grievances against King George III and asserts that the colonists have the right to govern themselves. It also outlines the principles of liberty and equality that would inspire future generations.

In summary, the Declaration of Independence was a groundbreaking document that declared the independence of the American colonies from British rule. It asserted the rights of the colonists and laid the foundation for the formation of the United States of America. The document has had a lasting impact on our nation and is celebrated every year on July 4th as Independence Day.
