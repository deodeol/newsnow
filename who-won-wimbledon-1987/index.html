What is Wimbledon and its significance?

Wimbledon is the oldest tennis tournament in the world, dating back to 1877. Held annually in London, England, it is one of the four Grand Slam tournaments, along with the Australian Open, French Open, and US Open. The tournament is renowned for its traditional grass courts and strict dress code, which adds to its unique charm. Wimbledon holds a special place in the hearts of tennis enthusiasts and players alike, representing the pinnacle of the sport.
