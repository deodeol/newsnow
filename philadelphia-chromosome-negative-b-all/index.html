What is Philadelphia Chromosome Negative B-ALL?

Philadelphia Chromosome Negative B-ALL, or Ph- B-ALL, is a type of acute lymphoblastic leukemia (ALL) that does not have the Philadelphia chromosome mutation. This mutation, which is present in about 25% of ALL cases, is associated with a more aggressive form of the disease. Ph- B-ALL typically affects B-cells, a type of white blood cell that plays a crucial role in the immune system.

Philadelphia Chromosome Negative B-ALL is characterized by the absence of the Philadelphia chromosome mutation, which is typically associated with a poorer prognosis.
