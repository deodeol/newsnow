Ph Chromosome In All: Explained

The Ph chromosome, also known as the Philadelphia chromosome, was first discovered in 1960 by researchers Peter Nowell and David Hungerford. It is a genetic abnormality that results from a translocation between chromosomes 9 and 22. This translocation leads to the fusion of two genes, BCR and ABL1, creating a hybrid gene known as BCR-ABL1. This fusion gene produces a protein with abnormal tyrosine kinase activity, which plays a crucial role in the development of CML.

CML is a type of blood cancer that affects the bone marrow and results in the overproduction of immature white blood cells. The presence of the Ph chromosome in CML patients is a hallmark of the disease and has important diagnostic implications. Testing for the presence of the Ph chromosome is done through cytogenetic analysis or molecular techniques such as fluorescence in situ hybridization (FISH) or polymerase chain reaction (PCR).

The discovery of the Ph chromosome was a groundbreaking moment in the field of genetics.
