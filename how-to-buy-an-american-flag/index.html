What is an American flag?

The American flag is a symbol of the United States and its values. It consists of thirteen horizontal stripes, alternating between red and white, representing the original thirteen colonies. In the top left corner, there is a blue rectangle, known as the "union," which contains fifty white stars, representing the fifty states. The flag is a powerful symbol of American patriotism and is often displayed during holidays, events, and in front of homes and businesses.

History and Myth of the American flag

The American flag has a rich history that dates back to the Revolutionary War. The flag has gone through several design changes over the years, with the current design being adopted in 1960. There are also various myths and legends surrounding the flag, such as the story of Betsy Ross sewing the first American flag. While the exact origins of the flag may be debated, its significance and symbolism remain strong.

How to properly display an American flag

To properly display an American flag, follow these guidelines:

  • When hanging the flag vertically, the union (blue rectangle) should be at the top and to the left of the viewer
  • When hanging the flag horizontally, the union should be on the viewer's left
  • If displaying the flag at night, it should be properly illuminated
