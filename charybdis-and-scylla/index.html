The History and Myth of Charybdis and Scylla

The story of Charybdis and Scylla dates back to ancient Greek mythology. According to legend, Charybdis was once a beautiful nymph who angered the god Zeus. As punishment, she was transformed into a monstrous whirlpool that swallowed everything in its path. Scylla, on the other hand, was a sea nymph who was transformed into a six-headed monster by the witch Circe. Together, these creatures became a formidable duo, terrorizing sailors who dared to venture into their waters.

Over the centuries, the tale of Charybdis and Scylla has been retold and reimagined in various forms of literature and art.
