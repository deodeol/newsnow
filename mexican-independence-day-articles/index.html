Fun Facts about Mexican Independence Day Articles

- Mexican Independence Day is celebrated on September 16th, which is the anniversary of the start of the war for independence in 1810. - The cry of independence, known as "El Grito," is a significant part of the celebrations. It is a reenactment of Miguel Hidalgo's call to arms and is shouted by the President of Mexico on the night of September 15th. - Traditional Mexican foods, such as tamales, pozole, and chiles en nogada, are often enjoyed during the celebrations. - Mexican Independence Day is a national holiday in Mexico, and celebrations take place throughout the country, including parades, fireworks, and cultural performances. - The colors of the Mexican flag - green, white, and red - are prominently displayed during the celebrations, symbolizing hope, purity, and the blood shed by the heroes of the independence movement.
