Direction to Coney Island: History and Myth

Coney Island has a rich history and is filled with myths and legends. It was originally inhabited by the Lenape Native Americans, who called it "Narrioch" meaning "land without shadows." In the late 19th century, Coney Island became a popular resort destination for New Yorkers looking to escape the city's heat.

One of the most famous myths surrounding Coney Island is the legend of the Mermaid Parade. According to the myth, the Mermaid Parade was started by a group of Russian immigrants who wanted to celebrate their heritage. Today, the Mermaid Parade is an annual event that attracts thousands of people from all over the world.

Another popular myth is the story of the Coney Island Circus Sideshow. According to legend, the circus was founded in the early 20th century and featured a variety of strange and unusual acts. While the original circus no longer exists, there are still sideshow performers who put on shows at Coney Island.
