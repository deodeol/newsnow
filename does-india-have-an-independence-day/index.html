The History and Myth of India's Independence Day

India's struggle for independence began in the late 19th century with the formation of various organizations and movements aimed at achieving freedom from British rule. One of the most significant events in India's struggle for independence was the Salt March, led by Mahatma Gandhi in 1930. It was a nonviolent protest against the British salt tax, and it sparked a wave of civil disobedience across the country. The Salt March and other acts of nonviolent resistance played a crucial role in gaining international support for India's independence movement.
