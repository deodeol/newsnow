History and Myth of Robert De Niro Movies Ranked

Robert De Niro's career began in the late 1960s, where he gained recognition for his roles in films like "Mean Streets" and "Bang the Drum Slowly." However, it was his collaboration with director Martin Scorsese in the 1970s that truly launched him into stardom. Movies like "Taxi Driver," "Raging Bull," and "Goodfellas" showcased De Niro's immense talent and solidified his status as one of the greatest actors of his generation.

Over the years, De Niro continued to deliver outstanding performances in films of various genres. He has worked with some of the most celebrated directors in the industry, including Francis Ford Coppola, Sergio Leone, and David O. Russell. His ability to bring depth and complexity to his characters has made him a sought-after actor in Hollywood.

With such an illustrious career, it's no wonder that myths and legends have formed around Robert De Niro's movies. From stories of his intense method acting to rumors of on-set antics, De Niro's films have become the stuff of Hollywood folklore. These myths only add to the allure of his movies and make them even more fascinating to watch.

Recommendations for Robert De Niro Movies Ranked

If you're new to Robert De Niro's filmography, it can be overwhelming to decide where to start. Here are some recommendations to get you started on your journey through his movies:

  1. "Taxi Driver" (1976) - Directed by Martin Scorsese, this film is a dark and gritty exploration of urban alienation, with De Niro delivering a mesmerizing performance as Travis Bickle, a mentally unstable taxi driver.
  2. "Raging Bull" (1980) - De Niro gained 60 pounds and trained as a boxer to play real-life boxer Jake LaMotta in this Martin Scorsese-directed drama. The film showcases De Niro's commitment to his craft and is considered one of his finest performances.
  3. "Goodfellas" (1990) - Directed by Martin Scorsese, this crime film follows the rise and fall of Henry Hill, played by Ray Liotta. De Niro's portrayal of mobster Jimmy Conway is a standout performance in an ensemble cast.
  4. "Heat" (1995) - Directed by Michael Mann, this crime thriller pairs De Niro with Al Pacino as two men on opposite sides of the law. The film features an iconic face-off scene between the two actors and is a must-watch for any fan of De Niro.
  5. "Silver Linings Playbook" (2012) - In this romantic comedy-drama, De Niro plays the father of a man struggling with bipolar disorder. His performance earned him an Academy Award nomination and showcases his versatility as an actor.

These recommendations are just a starting point, and there are many more incredible films in De Niro's filmography to explore.
