Koh Samui Thailand Airport: A Gateway to Paradise

Koh Samui Thailand Airport, also known as Samui International Airport, is a privately-owned airport located on the northeastern coast of Koh Samui. It serves as the main gateway to the island, connecting it to domestic and international destinations. The airport is operated by Bangkok Airways, which offers numerous flights to and from popular destinations like Bangkok, Phuket, Chiang Mai, and Singapore.

Upon arrival, you'll be greeted by the airport's stunning architecture, inspired by traditional Thai design and infused with modern elements. The open-air terminal and lush tropical surroundings create a unique ambiance that immediately transports you to the paradise that is Koh Samui.

The History and Myth of Koh Samui Thailand Airport

Koh Samui Thailand Airport was first opened in 1989 and has since undergone several expansions and renovations to accommodate the growing number of tourists visiting the island. It has played a significant role in boosting tourism and economic development in Koh Samui, making it one of the most popular destinations in Thailand.

As for myths, there is a popular belief among locals that Koh Samui Thailand Airport is built on an ancient burial ground. According to legend, the spirits of the deceased protect the airport and bring good luck to travelers. Whether you believe in these stories or not, the airport's serene and welcoming atmosphere will certainly make you feel blessed.
